01. The Author

Nic Stone is from Atlanta, USA. She used to work with teenagers.

She wrote Dear Martin in 2017. She wanted to show the hard life of young Black men in America.

Why she wrote it:
Nic Stone has two sons. She wrote this book for them. She asked: "What would Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. do today?"

02. Summary

The Problem

Justyce McAllister is 17 years old. He is very smart and wants to go to Yale University. One night, he helps his ex-girlfriend because she is in trouble. A police officer sees him and thinks he is doing something bad. The officer arrests Justyce violently because he is Black. This event makes Justyce very sad and afraid of the world.

The Letters

Justyce is angry and confused about how he was treated. He starts writing letters to Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in his diary. He wants to know if being peaceful still works today. He tries to be a good person every day. However, it is very hard because students at school say mean things about him. He feels like he does not belong anywhere anymore.

The Tragedy

Later, Justyce and his best friend Manny are driving in a car. They are happy and playing loud music. A white man in another car gets very angry about the music. The man shouts mean words and then shoots a gun at them. Manny is killed, and Justyce is hurt very badly. This tragic event changes Justyce's life and his view of justice forever.

04. Main Topics

Racism

The book shows how people are treated badly because of skin color. It talks about unfair police actions in the streets. People often judge Justyce before they even know him. This makes life very difficult for young Black men today.

Identity

Justyce asks: "Who am I?" He feels too "Black" for his white school. He feels too "white" for his old neighborhood. He tries to find his own way to be a man in a world that is often unfair.

05. The Sequel: Dear Justyce

Nic Stone wrote a second book called Dear Justyce. This book is about Quan, who is Manny's cousin. Quan is in prison for a crime he did not really do. He writes letters to Justyce to explain his hard life.

This story shows how the legal system is very hard for young Black men. It tells us why some boys end up in gangs. It is a very emotional story about friendship and support. You should read it after the first book!
